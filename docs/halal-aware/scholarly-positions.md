# Scholarly positions

This page exists to keep the [halal-aware modes](./index.md) honest: every mode must trace to a
**documented position with a citation**, reviewed by a qualified advisor before it ships.

> **This is a placeholder pending scholarly review.** The text below describes the _kind_ of content
> that belongs here and must be completed with proper sourcing and advisor sign-off per
> [GOVERNANCE.md](../../GOVERNANCE.md). Do not ship user-facing claims from this page until that
> review is done.

## What each entry must contain

For every mode, this page should record:

1. **The position**, stated neutrally (e.g. "the duff is permitted, melodic instruments are not").
2. **Who holds it** — schools/scholars, with references to primary or well-attested secondary
   sources.
3. **The strongest counter-position**, stated fairly, so users see this is a matter of legitimate
   difference.
4. **What Sukoon does** for that mode, mechanically, and its limitations (e.g. separation can't be
   perfect on dense mixes).

## Modes to document

- **`remove-all`** — the position that instrumental music is to be avoided. _(default; chosen as the
  broadest common denominator, not as an endorsement.)_ **Implemented today.**
- **`keep-vocals`** — positions on unaccompanied vocal content / human voice. On the current 2-stem
  engine this resolves to the same behaviour as `remove-all` (keep the voice, drop the instrumental).
- **`keep-percussion`** — the position permitting the duff/hand-drum while excluding melodic,
  stringed, and wind instruments. _Not implemented — needs a multi-stem engine (future)._
- **`preserve-effects`** — a practical (non-fiqh) distinction: ambient sound and effects are not
  "music." _Not implemented — needs a multi-stem engine (future)._

## Principles for this page

- Present **multiple positions**; never adjudicate between scholars.
- Cite sources; avoid paraphrase that distorts.
- Keep the language accessible and humble. The goal is informed choice, not persuasion.
